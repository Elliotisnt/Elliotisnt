## Hi, I'm Elliot

Data science student at Purdue Fort Wayne. I work on NSF-funded research applying SINDy (Sparse Identification of Nonlinear Dynamics) to discover governing equations in dynamical systems. Our first paper, ["On the Effectiveness of Sparse Identification Methods to Detect Nonlinear Models of Oscillatory Dynamics in Psychology and the Life Sciences"](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41410560/), was recently published in *Nonlinear Dynamics, Psychology, and Life Sciences*.

Outside of research, I like building things that solve problems I actually have: a [macro indicator monitor](https://github.com/elliotisnt/bretton-woods-decay) for retirement allocation decisions, a [Pi-hole DNS server](https://github.com/elliotisnt/dietpi-pihole) on a repurposed school laptop, and a containerized media stack on a 2010 Mac Pro I saved from the scrapyard.

I'm applying to Statistics/Biostatistics PhD programs for Fall 2026.
